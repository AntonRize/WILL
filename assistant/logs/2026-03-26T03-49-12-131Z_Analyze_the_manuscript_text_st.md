### 4. Domain Boundaries

The manuscript primarily claims universal applicability and deep generative power, but it does specify conditions and contexts that imply boundaries for its simplified forms or a hierarchy of applicability:

*   **Energetic Closure:** The derivation of the fundamental closure law $\kappa^2=2\beta^2$ is specific to "Closed systems (momentary or periodic)" and notes that "Open systems display $\delta \neq 1$, the magnitude of which quantifies the energy flow through unaccounted channels." [WILL RG Part I - Energetic Closure Criterion](\hypertarget{cor:closurecriterion}). This implies that for open systems, the simple closure law requires modification or a more comprehensive accounting of energy channels.
*   **Weak-field Approximations vs. Exact Forms:** The text distinguishes between exact algebraic forms and weak-field approximations, particularly in the context of orbital precession: "This confirms that the classical tensor calculus results, which cost years of study to master, are merely truncated shadows of the exact relational phase geometry." [WILL RG Part I - Legacy Translation: Recovering the General Relativity Approximation](\hypertarget{sec:precession_law}). This implies that standard approximations are valid only within certain limits.
*   **Non-relativistic approximation for atomic structure:** The derivation of atomic structure explicitly uses a "non-relativistic approximation for $\beta_q$" [WILL RG Part III - Derivation of Atomic Structure from First Principles](\hypertarget{sec:atomic-structure}), while acknowledging that "non-relativistic models and simple relativistic corrections fail to describe the fine structure of high-Z atoms." This indicates a limitation of the specific set of equations used for the basic atomic structure derivation, requiring the more complete "Unified Relativistic Energy Formula" for fine structure [WILL RG Part III - The Unified Relativistic Energy Formula](\hypertarget{thm:unified-energy}).
*   **Classical limit for Lagrangian/Hamiltonian:** The Lagrangian and Hamiltonian formalisms "arise as limiting cases of the two-point relational Energy-Symmetry Law" when collapsed into a single-point description [WILL RG Part I - Lagrangian and Hamiltonian as Single-Point Limits of the Relational Energy-Symmetry Law](\hypertarget{sec:LH}). *   **Neutrino Exclusion for Hubble Parameter:** The derivation of $H_0$ from CMB temperature and $\alpha$ explicitly excludes neutrinos, stating: "Therefore, including the neutrino background ($\rho_\nu$) in the definition of an $\alpha$-governed horizon would constitute a category error."
