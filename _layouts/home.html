COSMOS ≡ LOGOS ≡ GEOMETRY

Methodological Pillars


1. NO ONTOLOGICAL COMMITMENTS

This model makes no claims about the "existence" of space, particles, or fields as independent entities. Instead, all phenomena are treated as observer-dependent energetic projections. Physics is derived from the relational structure between observers, governed by the foundational Energy Symmetry Law: $$\Delta E_{A \rightarrow B} + \Delta E_{B \rightarrow A} = 0$$ This ensures that energy is always conserved and redistributed in any interaction, never created or destroyed.

2. EPISTEMIC HYGIENE

WILL Geometry enforces a disciplined refusal to introduce unjustified assumptions or hidden parameters. The guiding principle is: Nothing is assumed. Everything is derived. All physical quantities, including space, time, mass, and energy, must emerge directly from relational geometry and causal structure, eliminating the need for arbitrary constants or abstract entities.

3. STRICT RELATIONALISM

All physical quantities are defined purely by their relations to one another, not by absolute properties or external frames. The framework disallows all background structures, even asymptotic ones, which are often implicitly used in other theories. Energy itself is not a substance but a differential invariant of state transitions within the WILL framework.

4. ZERO FREE PARAMETERS

The framework introduces no free parameters. All measurable quantities and fundamental "constants"—from the cosmological density parameter \( \Omega_\Lambda \) to the equation of state \(w = -1\)—emerge as necessary consequences of the geometric and algebraic closure of the system. They are not fitted to data, but are derived from first principles.

5. STRUCTURAL DYNAMICS

The system is not described by differential equations of motion evolving in time. Instead, its evolution is dictated by a closed network of algebraic relations that enforce a perpetually balanced configuration. Any change in one parameter necessitates a coordinated shift in all others to maintain geometric self-consistency. What we perceive as "dynamics" is this ordered succession of balanced states. The framework thus inverts the classical paradigm: Time does not drive change — change defines time.

6. GEOMETRIC NECESSITY

There is only one valid configuration for a system at any moment: the one where all projectional constraints are satisfied. Everything else is not forbidden—it is geometrically and topologically undefined. The universe does not "choose" a path that minimizes action; it simply exists in the state of perfect balance. Causality is internal, coherence is enforced, and the laws of physics are an inevitable consequence of the system's self-consistency.
