# WILL — Sources, Data & References (Draft v0.1)

> This page collects the external sources, datasets, constants, and classic results that Anton’s **predictions** and **results** pages rely on. The goal is to have a single, citable hub that you can link to from `/predictions/` and `/results/` (and from individual notebooks/calculators). Each entry has a suggested short tag you can reuse as an inline anchor link, e.g. `[SPARC2016](/refs/#sparc2016)`.

---

## 0) How to use this page in-site

* Place a small **“Sources”** block at the bottom of each section on `/predictions/` and `/results/` with shorttags linked here. Example:

  `Sources: [SPARC2016](/refs/#sparc2016), [MtoL2014](/refs/#mtol2014), [Ashby2003](/refs/#ashby2003)`

* Keep **code/data** repo links next to each figure; keep **papers** here.

* For new claims, add the paper below once and link by shorttag.

## 1) Datasets & Data Releases

### [SPARC2016]{#sparc2016}

**Lelli, F.; McGaugh, S.S.; Schombert, J.M.** (2016). *SPARC: Mass Models for 175 Disk Galaxies with Spitzer Photometry.* **The Astronomical Journal** 152, 157. DOI: 10.3847/0004-6256/152/6/157.

* Primary dataset for rotation curves & 3.6μm photometry; 175 galaxies.
* Data hub: CDS ReadMe, mirrors, and SPARC project page.

### [BTF2012]{#btf2012}

**McGaugh, S.S.** (2012). *The Baryonic Tully–Fisher Relation of Gas–Rich Galaxies as a Test of ΛCDM and MOND.* **AJ** 143, 40. DOI: 10.1088/0004-6256/143/2/40.

* If you reference the BTF anywhere, link this.

### [MtoL2014]{#mtol2014}

**McGaugh, S.S.; Schombert, J.M.** (2014). *Color–Mass-to-Light Ratio Relations for Disk Galaxies.* **AJ** 148, 77. DOI: 10.1088/0004-6256/148/4/77.

* Common source for ~constant M/L at 3.6 μm used in mass models.

## 3) Classic GR Results Used for Checks/Comparisons

### Light Deflection / Solar Eclipse Tests

#### [Will2018]{#will2018}

**Will, C.M.** (2018). *The Confrontation between General Relativity and Experiment.* **Living Reviews in Relativity** 21, 3. DOI: 10.1007/s41114-018-0017-9.

* Summary of experimental status; includes (\alpha = 4GM/(bc^2)) baseline.

#### [Ashby2003]{#ashby2003}

**Ashby, N.** (2003). *Relativity in the Global Positioning System.* **Living Reviews in Relativity** 6, 1. DOI: 10.12942/lrr-2003-1.

* Standard reference for GPS time-dilation & gravitational redshift totals (~38 μs/day).

### Mercury Perihelion Precession

#### [Will2018-Mercury]{#will2018mercury}

Use Will (2018) or a modern GR textbook citation for (\Delta \varpi) formulae. If you prefer a primary, add Einstein’s 1915 note; otherwise Will is fine.

### Photon Sphere & ISCO (Schwarzschild)

#### [Claudel2001]{#claudel2001}

**Claudel, C.-M.; Virbhadra, K.S.; Ellis, G.F.R.** (2001). *The Geometry of Photon Surfaces.* **J. Math. Phys.** 42, 818–838. DOI: 10.1063/1.1308507.

* Formal treatment of photon surfaces; for Schwarzschild gives (r_{\rm ph}=3M) ((=1.5,R_s)).

#### [BPT1972]{#bpt1972}

**Bardeen, J.M.; Press, W.H.; Teukolsky, S.A.** (1972). *Rotating Black Holes: Locally Nonrotating Frames, Energy Extraction, and Scalar Synchrotron Radiation.* **ApJ** 178, 347.

* Often cited for ISCO & Kerr properties; use alongside Kerr (1963) below.

### Schwarzschild & Kerr Primaries

#### [Schwarzschild1916]{#schwarzschild1916}

**Schwarzschild, K.** (1916). *On the Gravitational Field of a Mass Point.* (English translations available).

#### [Kerr1963]{#kerr1963}

**Kerr, R.P.** (1963). *Gravitational Field of a Spinning Mass as an Example of Algebraically Special Metrics.* **Phys. Rev. Lett.** 11, 237. DOI: 10.1103/PhysRevLett.11.237.

#### [BoyerLindquist1967]{#bl1967}

**Boyer, R.H.; Lindquist, R.W.** (1967). *Maximal Analytic Extension of the Kerr Metric.* **J. Math. Phys.** 8, 265.

### Stellar Structure / TOV (for interior (g_{rr}) discussion in Appendix)

#### [Tolman1939]{#tolman1939}

**Tolman, R.C.** (1939). *Static Solutions of Einstein’s Field Equations for Spheres of Fluid.* **Phys. Rev.** 55, 364. DOI: 10.1103/PhysRev.55.364.

#### [OV1939]{#ov1939}

**Oppenheimer, J.R.; Volkoff, G.M.** (1939). *On Massive Neutron Cores.* **Phys. Rev.** 55, 374. DOI: 10.1103/PhysRev.55.374.

## 4) Timing Tests / Binary Pulsars / LLR

### Hulse–Taylor Binary (PSR B1913+16)

#### [WeisbergHuang2016]{#weisberghuang2016}

**Weisberg, J.M.; Huang, Y.** (2016). *Relativistic Measurements from Timing the Binary Pulsar PSR B1913+16.* **ApJ** 829, 55. DOI: 10.3847/0004-637X/829/1/55. (arXiv:1606.02744)

* Reports observed (\dot P) / GR-predicted ratio (0.9983\pm0.0016).

### Double Pulsar (PSR J0737–3039A/B)

#### [Kramer2021]{#kramer2021}

**Kramer, M., et al.** (2021). *Strong-Field Gravity Tests with the Double Pulsar.* **Phys. Rev. X** 11, 041050. DOI: 10.1103/PhysRevX.11.041050. (arXiv:2112.06795)

* High-precision PK tests; validates GR’s quadrupolar radiation to (1.3\times10^{-4}).

### Lunar Laser Ranging (LLR) — Recession and Tidal Power

#### [Dickey1994]{#dickey1994}

**Dickey, J.O., et al.** (1994). *Lunar Laser Ranging: A Continuing Legacy of the Apollo Program.* **Science** 265, 482–490. DOI: 10.1126/science.265.5171.482.

* Classic LLR overview.

#### [Williams2016]{#williams2016}

**Williams, J.G.; Boggs, D.H.** (2016). *Tides on the Moon: Theory and Determination of Dissipation.* **JGR Planets** 121, 688–705. DOI: 10.1002/2015JE004559.

* Recession rate (~38,\mathrm{mm,yr^{-1}}); use for Earth–Moon energy flow.
