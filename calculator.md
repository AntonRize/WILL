  <h3 class="text-lg font-bold text-blue-400 mb-2">Baryonic Mass Model</h3>
  <p class="text-gray-300 mb-4">
    The baryonic velocity \(V_{bary}\) is constructed strictly from the observed mass components provided by the SPARC catalog (Lelli et al., 2016). We allow for standard stellar population uncertainty via Mass-to-Light ratios (\(\Upsilon_*\)):
  </p>
  <div class="bg-gray-900/50 p-4 rounded border-l-4 border-blue-500 mb-6">
    $$V_{bary}(r) = \sqrt{V_{gas}^2(r) + \Upsilon_{disk} V_{disk}^2(r) + \Upsilon_{bulge} V_{bulge}^2(r)}$$
  </div>

  <h3 class="text-lg font-bold text-blue-400 mb-2">Physics-Based Error Floor</h3>
  <p class="text-gray-300 mb-4">
    Galactic gas disks are turbulent systems. While catalog errors are often quoted as instrumental precision (1–2 km/s), the physical reality involves turbulence, warps, and non-circular motions.
    To avoid "overfitting the noise" and to compare our fundamental law against physical reality, we apply a systematic <b>Error Floor</b>:
  </p>
  <div class="bg-gray-800 p-4 rounded-lg font-mono text-sm text-green-300 mb-8">
    \(\sigma_{used} = \max(\sigma_{obs}, \ 5.0 \text{ km/s}, \ 0.05 \cdot |V_{obs}|)\)
  </div>
