\section{Derivation of the Baryonic Tully-Fisher Relation}
\hypertarget{sec:Tully-Fisher}{}\label{sec:Tully-Fisher}

The Baryonic Tully-Fisher Relation (BTFR) is one of the tightest empirical correlations in extragalactic astronomy: the asymptotic rotation velocity of a disk galaxy scales as $V_{flat}^4 \propto M_b$, where $M_b$ is the total baryonic mass. We derive this scaling, including its slope and normalization, directly from the Resonant Interference established in Section~\ref{sec:galactic-dynamics}.

\subsection{Asymptotic Limit of the Horizon Interference}

The total observed velocity at radius $r$ from a baryonic mass $M_b$ is given by the Resonant Interference equation:
\begin{equation}
V_{obs}^2(r) = V_N^2(r) + \sqrt{V_N^2(r) \cdot a_{\kappa} \cdot r},
\label{eq:V_obs_TF}
\end{equation}
where $V_N^2 = GM_b/r$ is the Newtonian circular velocity for a point mass, and $a_{\kappa} = cH_0/(3\pi)$ is the structural resonance scale.

A key algebraic property of this equation emerges for the point-mass limit. The interference term simplifies to:
\begin{equation}
\sqrt{V_N^2 \cdot a_{\kappa} \cdot r} = \sqrt{\frac{GM_b}{r} \cdot a_{\kappa} \cdot r} = \sqrt{GM_b \cdot a_{\kappa}} = \text{const}.
\end{equation}
The $r$-dependence cancels identically. The Horizon interference contribution is therefore a \textbf{constant velocity increment} at all radii, which provides a natural mechanism for asymptotically flat rotation curves.

The full velocity profile for a point mass thus takes the form:
\begin{equation}
V_{obs}^2(r) = \frac{GM_b}{r} + \sqrt{GM_b \cdot a_{\kappa}}.
\end{equation}
As $r \to \infty$, the Newtonian term vanishes, yielding the asymptotic flat velocity:
\begin{equation}
V_{flat}^2 = (GM_b \cdot a_{\kappa})^{1/2}.
\end{equation}

Squaring both sides gives the \textbf{Baryonic Tully-Fisher Relation}:
\begin{equation}
\boxed{V_{flat}^4 = G \cdot a_{\kappa} \cdot M_b}
\label{eq:BTFR}
\end{equation}
