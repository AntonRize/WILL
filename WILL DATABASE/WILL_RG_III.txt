\section{Geometric Origin of the de Broglie Relation }

We begin with derivation of the de Broglie Relation which build on the results of WILL Part I

\begin{theorem}[Geometric Origin of the de Broglie Relation]
On the $S^1$ kinematic manifold of WILL RG, let the horizontal (Amplitude) and vertical (Phase) projections be $(\beta,\beta_Y)$ with

\[

\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Derivation from $S^1$ Geometry]

\[
p = \frac{E}{c}\,\beta = \frac{E_0}{c}\,\frac{\beta}{\beta_Y}.

\[

for some calibration length $\Lambda_0$ that fixes the map from dimensionless phase to physical length.

\textbf{(3) Product.} Multiplying,
\[
p\,\lambda = \left(\frac{E_0}{c}\,\frac{\beta}{\beta_Y}\right)\left(\Lambda_0\,\frac{\beta_Y}{\beta}\right)
= \frac{E_0}{c}\,\Lambda_0.

The projections cancel identically, so $p\lambda$ is a constant independent of state.

\textbf{(4) Calibration to $h$.} Choosing the phase–length calibration so that one full $2\pi$ winding on $S^1$ corresponds to one quantum of action,
\[

\]
we obtain the standard de Broglie relation $\boxed{p\,\lambda = h}$.
\end{proof}
