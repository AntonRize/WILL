\section{Stage 0: The Last Geocentric Epicycle}
\hypertarget{sec:epicycle}{}\label{sec:epicycle}

General Relativity remains the most successful description of gravitation we possess, and its author regarded the elimination of background structure as central to its meaning: Einstein insisted that space-time ``does not claim existence on its own, but only as a structural quality of the field.'' The analysis below is therefore aimed not at General Relativity itself, but at the scaffolding it inherited. What follows is offered as the next step in GR's own evolution --- an attempt to complete the relational program Einstein began.

The standard formulation of General Relativity relies on the concept of a pre-existing, container-like spacetime that then gets â€śfilledâ€ť with fields and matter.

Derivation of the Einstein field equations begins with the Einstein-Hilbert action, which is built upon the metric as the fundamental variable. This metric is defined on a smooth manifold that is assumed to exist \textit{a priori}. This manifold, even without a metric, carries topological and differential structure - an absolute scaffold. This constitutes surplus ontology because it introduces an entity (the manifold) that is neither derived nor observed.

The stress-energy tensor is derived from the variation of the matter Lagrangian with respect to the metric. This assumes that energy is a property of matter fields that can be localized in spacetime. However, this localization is frame-dependent (via the equivalence principle) and leads to well-known problems such as the non-uniqueness of the gravitational energy-momentum pseudotensor. Energy is treated as an absolute property of matter rather than a relational measure. This remains a metaphysical assumption at the heart of modern physics.

\subsection{The contemporary split: an inherited ontological commitment}
All present-day theories (SR, GR, QFT, $\Lambda$CDM, Standard Model) are
built with a \emph{bi-variable} syntax:
\[
\underbrace{\text{fixed manifold + metric}}_{\text{structure}}
\quad+\quad
\underbrace{\text{fields + constants}}_{\text{dynamics}}.
\]
No observation demands this duplication; it is retained purely because the resulting Lagrangians are empirically adequate \emph{inside} the split.  The split is therefore \emph{not} an empirical discovery but an inherited, untested ontological assumption.

\subsection{Empirical deficit of the separation}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Local energy conservation} verified only \emph{after} the
        metric is declared fixed; no experiment varies the \emph{volume} of
        flat space and checks calorimetry.
  \item \textbf{Universality of free fall} tests $m_i=m_g$ numerically, not
        the claim that inertia resides \emph{in} the object rather than in
        a geometric scaling relation.
  \item \textbf{Gravitational-wave polarisations} test spin content, not
        ontology; extra modes can still be called ``matter on spacetime''.
  \item \textbf{Casimir/Lamb shifts} measure \emph{differences} of vacuum
        energy between two geometries; the absolute bulk term is explicitly
        subtracted, leaving the split intact.
\end{itemize}

Each of these tests is an \emph{internal consistency check} of a formalism that already presupposes two substances.
None constitutes \emph{positive evidence} for the split.

\subsection{Historical Pattern: breakthroughs delete, not add}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Copernicus} eliminated the Earth/cosmos separation.
  \item \textbf{Newton} eliminated the terrestrial/celestial law separation.
  \item \textbf{Maxwell} eliminated the electricity/magnetism separation.
  \item   \textbf{Einstein} eliminated the space/time separation.
\end{itemize}

Each step widened the relational circle and reduced the number of primitives --- the unexplained absolutes.
The spacetime--energy split is the only survivor of this pruning sequence.

\subsection{Consequence}
Until an experiment varies the amount of space while holding everything else fixed, 
the spacetime--energy separation remains an \emph{untested metaphysical postulate}.
\textbf{It is the last geocentric epicycle in physics.} Removing it is not a rejection of General Relativity; it is the same pruning operation that produced General Relativity in the first place.

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=gray!5, colframe=black!80!black, title=A Note on Methodology vs. Ontology]
It may be objected that the foundational principles constitute axioms, thereby contradicting the claim of \textit{zero assumptions}. This objection conflates \textit{methodological constraints} with \textit{ontological axioms}. 

Under the strictures of the Scientific Method, the burden of proof rests exclusively on the claimant asserting an absolute existence. Refusing to import unobserved absolutes (such as a background container or intrinsic properties) is not an assumption; it is the default epistemic stance --- the null hypothesis. 

These principles function strictly as rules of logical engagement to prevent the introduction of surplus ontology. To challenge them is to argue for the admission of a specific unobserved absolute --- and under the empirical foundation of physics, the burden of proof rests with that admission.
\end{tcolorbox}

\subsubsection{The Foundational Core: Relational Geometry}
\hypertarget{sec:core}{}\label{sec:core}

These foundational methodological principles lead us to the core of Relational Geometry:

\begin{theorem}[Relational Closure]
\hypertarget{thm:relational_closure}{}\label{thm:relational_closure}
\leavevmode
A purely relational system cannot be geometrically open; any stable, self-contained system must be described by a closed and self-consistent relational geometry. Its geometry is closed by absolute analytic necessity. 
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof by Analytic Tautology]
\leavevmode
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Premise 1 (Relational Origin):} Within RG Physical quantities are defined exclusively by their relations (Principle \ref{pr:relational}).
    \item \textbf{Premise 2 (Interaction as Integration):} If a hypothetical ``outside'' entity or boundary were to physically interact with the system, that interaction itself constitutes a relation.
    \item \textbf{Deduction (The Tautology):} As relation is formed, it is mathematically and physically integrated into the system. 
\end{enumerate}
An ``open relational system'' experiencing external influence is a logical contradiction. The system is closed not by a spatial boundary or a metric container, but by the strict impossibility of an interacting non-relation. The system is relationally closed by definition.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Causal Continuity (Conservation)]
\hypertarget{thm:causal_continuity}{}\label{thm:causal_continuity}
\leavevmode
 Within any closed dynamical system there must exist an invariant quantitative measure of change. Without an external background any change must be perfectly balanced by a complementary change elsewhere in the system enforcing \textbf{Causal Continuity}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof by Structural Necessity]
\leavevmode
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Premise 1 (Relational Origin):} Physical quantities are defined exclusively by their relations (Principle \ref{pr:relational}).
    \item \textbf{Premise 2 (Empirical Change):} States undergo observable change.
    \item \textbf{Premise 3 (Relational Closure):} As proven in Theorem \ref{thm:relational_closure}, the relational system is analytically closed. There is no external background, void, or hidden container to absorb or inject influence.
    \item \textbf{Deduction (Causal Continuity):} Because the system is closed (Premise 3), an empirical change (Premise 2) cannot vanish into a void or emerge from one. To maintain the structural integrity of the relations (Premise 1), any change must be perfectly balanced by a complementary change elsewhere in the system.
\end{enumerate}
Therefore, within this closed relational network, there must exist an invariant quantitative measure of change.
\end{proof}

\subsubsection{What is Energy in Relational Ontology?}

Across all domains of physics, one empirical fact persists: in every closed system there exists a quantity that never disappears or arises spontaneously, but only transforms in form. This invariant is observed under many guises --- kinetic, potential, thermal, chemical --- yet all are interchangeable, pointing to a single underlying structure.

Crucially, this quantity is never observed directly, but only through \textit{differences between states}: a change of velocity, a shift in configuration, a transition of phase. Its value is relational, not absolute: it depends on the chosen frame or comparison, never on an object in isolation.

\end{quote}

\begin{remark}[Energy Ledger: intuitive understanding]
\leavevmode
Consider a completely closed economy with no physical currency --- no dollar bills, no gold coins, and no central bank (acting as an absolute container). It is a purely relational economy consisting solely of a massive ledger of debts and credits between individuals. If one account decreases, another must increase. \subsubsection{Three Relational Energy Quantities}
\hypertarget{sec:energy_quantities}{}\label{sec:energy_quantities}

The formalism employs three distinct energies. Confusing them is the most common source of ambiguity, so we define and relate them explicitly. None of them presupposes a background frame; each is defined purely from the carrier closures.

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Kinematic carrier energy $E_{\beta}$:} From the invariant rest energy projection theorem (~\ref{thm:restenergy}), $E_{\beta}\,\beta_Y = E_0$, so $E_{\beta} = E_0/\beta_Y = E_0/\sin\theta_1$. This is the energy of a state whose only shift is kinematic ($\kappa = 0$). It reduces to the rest energy at $\beta = 0$ and diverges as $\beta \to 1$ (the speed-of-light horizon). It is the relational analogue of $\gamma m c^2$.

    \item \textbf{Potential carrier energy $E_{\kappa}$:} From the analogous invariant projection (Sec.~\ref{sec:potential}),
$E_{\kappa}\,\kappa_X = E_0$, so $E_{\kappa} = E_0/\kappa_X = E_0/\cos\theta_2$. This is the energy of a state whose only shift is gravitational 
($\beta = 0$). It reduces to the rest energy at $\kappa= 0$ and diverges as $\kappa \to 1$ (the causal horizon). Like $E_{\beta}$, it is a boosted carrier energy; it is not the energy of the combined state.

    \item \textbf{Relational total energy $E$:} When both carriers are active, the single conserved energy of the relation --- the quantity the Energy-Symmetry Law balances --- is the phase-ratio scaling $E = E_0\,\kappa_X/\beta_Y$.
\end{itemize}

The three are related by $E = E_0\,\kappa_X/\beta_Y = \kappa_X \cdot E_{\beta} = E_0 \cdot E_{\beta}/E_{\kappa}$, i.e.\ the relational energy is the kinematic carrier energy $E_{\beta}$ scaled by the potential phase $\kappa_X$ (equivalently, $E_0$ times the ratio of the two carrier energies). The relational total energy $E$ is the mixed quantity conserved by Causal Continuity Theorem (\ref{thm:causal_continuity}).

The limiting behavior is immediate: 
in the "flat" / no-gravity limit ($\kappa \to 0$), $E \to E_0/\beta_Y = E_{\beta}$ (pure kinematics); 
in the "static" / no relative motion limit ($\beta = 0$), $E = E_0\,\kappa_X = E_0\sqrt{1 - R_s/r}$, the familiar gravitational redshift factor. In this same limit the carrier energy is $E_{\kappa} = E_0/\kappa_X$, so $E \cdot E_{\kappa} = E_0^2$: the relational energy and the asymptotic carrier energy are reciprocal duals.

\subsubsection{Causal Continuity and Energy Symmetry}
\hypertarget{sec:energy-symmetry}{}\label{sec:energy-symmetry}

\begin{theorem}[Energy Symmetry]
Any closed system conserves its relational energy ledger with an invariant zero sum of total transformations. Transitions between states of observers (per unit of rest energy) must always sum to zero. Nothing is created, nothing is destroyed:
\begin{equation}
\Delta E_{A \to B} + \Delta E_{B \to A} = 0.
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By Theorems \textbf{Relational Closure} (~\ref{thm:relational_closure}) and \textbf{Causal Continuity} (~\ref{thm:causal_continuity}), the energy of a system is determined by the ratio of the potential phase to the kinematic phase:
\begin{equation}
E = E_0 \frac{\kappa_X}{\beta_Y}
\end{equation}
Consider two observers, $A$ and $B$. The specific energy difference perceived for a transition between their states is determined by the difference in their total phase scaling:
\begin{equation}
\Delta E_{A \to B} = E_0 \left( \frac{\kappa_{X,B}}{\beta_{Y,B}} - \frac{\kappa_{X,A}}{\beta_{Y,A}} \right)
\end{equation}
Conversely, the specific energy difference perceived for the reverse transition is:
\begin{equation}
\Delta E_{B \to A} = E_0 \left( \frac{\kappa_{X,A}}{\beta_{Y,A}} - \frac{\kappa_{X,B}}{\beta_{Y,B}} \right)
\end{equation}
Summing these transfers gives:
\begin{equation}
\Delta E_{A \to B} + \Delta E_{B \to A} = 0
\end{equation}
Thus, the Energy-Symmetry Law emerges directly from the phase ratio difference, ensuring causal continuity and unbroken ledger across all transitions.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[DOF-Indifference]

By maximal symmetry (no privileged directions, Theorem~\ref{thm:isotropy}) and ontological minimalism (no hidden structure, Principle~\ref{pr:minimalism}), each independent degree of freedom (DOF) has equal relational weight. WILL has no preferred directions and no preferred degrees of freedom.

Such a rule would constitute hidden structure, violating all four methodological principles (\ref{pr:relational}, \ref{pr:minimalism}, \ref{pr:mathematical}, \ref{pr:epistemic}). Therefore, each independent DOF must receive an identical relational weight $l$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Closure]
\hypertarget{thm:closure}{}\label{thm:closure}
In a relationally closed system, the kinematic carrier $S^1$ (1 DOF) and the potential carrier $S^2$ (2 DOF) exchange the active relational ledger in proportion to their degrees of freedom. Consequently, any relationally closed system must globally satisfy
$$ \boxed{\kappa^2 = 2\beta^2} $$

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Square amplitude on $S^1$ (1 DOF):}  $ \beta^2 = 1 \cdot l $
    \item \textbf{Square amplitude on $S^2$ (2 DOF):}  $ \kappa^2 = 2 \cdot l $
    \item \textbf{Substituting $l$ immediately yields closure:} $ \kappa^2 = 2\beta^2 $
\end{itemize}

\label{eq:delta-RG}
\end{equation}
A single bookkeeping parameter $\varepsilon$ is introduced via $\kappa_{E}^{2} \to \varepsilon\,\kappa_{E}^{2}$, $\beta_{E}^{2} \to \varepsilon\,\beta_{E}^{2}$, and the Taylor series of \eqref{eq:delta-RG} is computed to second order in $\varepsilon$ using \texttt{sympy.series}.
