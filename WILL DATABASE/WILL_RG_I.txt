\section{Stage 0: The Last Geocentric Epicycle}
\hypertarget{sec:epicycle}{}\label{sec:epicycle}

The standard formulation of General Relativity relies on the concept of a pre-existing, container-like spacetime that then gets “filled” with fields and matter.

Derivation of the Einstein field equations begins with the Einstein-Hilbert action, which is built upon the metric as the fundamental variable. This metric is defined on a smooth manifold that is assumed to exist \textit{a priori}. This manifold, even without a metric, carries topological and differential structure - an absolute scaffold. This constitutes surplus ontology because it introduces an entity (the manifold) that is not derived neither observed. 

The stress-energy tensor is derived from the variation of the matter Lagrangian with respect to the metric. This assumes that energy is a property of matter fields that can be localized in spacetime. However, this localization is frame-dependent (via the equivalence principle) and leads to well-known problems such as the non-uniqueness of the gravitational energy-momentum pseudotensor. Energy is treated as an absolute property of matter rather than a relational measure. This remains as metaphysical speculation in the heart of modern physics. 

\subsection{The contemporary split: an unjustified ontological commitment}
All present-day theories (SR, GR, QFT, CDM, Standard Model) are
built with a \emph{bi-variable} syntax:
\[
\underbrace{\text{fixed manifold + metric}}_{\text{structure}}
\quad+\quad
\underbrace{\text{fields + constants}}_{\text{dynamics}}.
\]
No observation demands this duplication; it is retained purely because the resulting Lagrangians are empirically adequate \emph{inside} the split.  The split is therefore \emph{not} an empirical discovery but an  unjustified ontological statement.

\subsection{Empirical deficit of the separation}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Local energy conservation} verified only \emph{after} the
        metric is declared fixed; no experiment varies the \emph{volume} of
        flat space and checks calorimetry.
  \item \textbf{Universality of free fall} tests $m_i=m_g$ numerically, not
        the claim that inertia resides \emph{in} the object rather than in
        a geometric scaling relation.
  \item \textbf{Gravitational-wave polarisations} test spin content, not
        ontology; extra modes can still be called ``matter on spacetime''.
  \item \textbf{Casimir/Lamb shifts} measure \emph{differences} of vacuum
        energy between two geometries; the absolute bulk term is explicitly
        subtracted, leaving the split intact.
\end{itemize}

Every ``test'' is an \emph{internal consistency check} of a formalism that already presupposes two substances.
None constitute \emph{positive evidence} for the split.

\subsection{Historical Pattern: breakthroughs delete, not add}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Copernicus} eliminated the Earth/cosmos separation.
  \item \textbf{Newton} eliminated the terrestrial/celestial law separation.
  \item \textbf{Maxwell} eliminated the electricity/magnetism separation.
  \item   \textbf{Einstein} eliminated the space/time separation.
\end{itemize}

Each step widened the relational circle and reduced the number of primitives - an unexplained absolutes.  
The spacetime--energy split is the only survivor of this pruning sequence.

Until an experiment varies the amount of space while holding everything else fixed,

\end{enumerate}

Ontological Minimalism and Mathematical Transparency uniquely enforce $S^1$ and $S^2$ as the minimal relational carriers without invoking metric geometry \emph{a priori}.
\end{proof}

\item Observer $B$ orbiting at radius $r_B > r_A$ with orbital velocity $v_B$ (state defined by $\kappa_B, \beta_B$).

\item It gains kinetic energy by accelerating to orbital velocity.

\boxed{\Delta E_{A \to B} = \underbrace{\frac{1}{2}(\kappa_A^2 - \kappa_B^2)}_{\text{Change in Potential}} + \underbrace{\frac{1}{2}(\beta_B^2 - \beta_A^2)}_{\text{Change in Kinetic}}}

\Delta E_{A \to B} = \frac{1}{2}((\kappa_A^2 - \kappa_B^2) + \beta_B^2)

From $B$'s perspective (transition from orbit to surface):
\begin{enumerate}
\item The object loses potential energy descending into a stronger gravitational field.
\item It loses kinetic energy by reducing its velocity to rest.

\Delta E_{B \to A} = \frac{1}{2}((\kappa_B^2 - \kappa_A^2) + (\beta_A^2 - \beta_B^2)) = \frac{1}{2}((\kappa_B^2 - \kappa_A^2) - \beta_B^2)
\end{equation}

Summing these transfers gives:
\begin{equation}
\Delta E_{A \to B} + \Delta E_{B \to A} = 0
\end{equation}
Thus, no net energy is created or destroyed in a closed cycle of transitions, confirming the Energy-Symmetry Law as a direct consequence of the closed geometry.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Two‑Point Origin of the Lagrangian and Hamiltonian}
\hypertarget{sec:LH}{}\label{sec:LH}

In the standard formulation of mechanics, the Lagrangian \(L = T - V\) and the Hamiltonian \(H = T + V\) are treated as fundamental functions of a single configuration point \(\mathbf{r}\).  The Relational Geometry

\subsubsection{The clean relational Lagrangian}

Choose a fixed reference state \(A\) – for instance, the surface of the central body – and consider a test body at a variable state \(B\) with velocity \(v\) and radial distance \(r\).  Since \(A\) is at rest on the surface,

\Delta E_{A \to B}

The quantity \(\Delta E_{A \to B}\) is the energy that must be supplied to move the test body from the surface to the orbit.  Interpreted as a Lagrangian, we may separate the kinetic and potential contributions and write the \textbf{relational Lagrangian.} The actual energy transfer is $  m c^2 \Delta E_{A\to B}  $. Substituting the definitions immediately yields $$m c^2 \Delta E_{A\to B} = \tfrac12 m v^2 + \tfrac12 m c^2 \kappa_A^2 - \frac{G M m}{r}$$ Interpreting the first two terms as the relational Lagrangian (with the constant surface term droppable) recovers the form used in mechanics.
\begin{equation}
L_{\rm rel} \;=\; \tfrac12 m v^{2} \;+\; \tfrac12 m c^{2} \kappa_A^{2} .
\label{eq:L_rel}
\end{equation}
The term \(\tfrac12 m c^{2} \kappa_A^{2}\) is a constant (the surface potential budget) and does not affect the equations of motion; it can be dropped.  Thus the clean relational Lagrangian is simply 
\(L_{\rm rel} = \tfrac12 m v^{2}\), which is the Newtonian kinetic energy alone – the entire potential information is carried by the two‑point difference, not by a pre‑assigned function \(V(r)\).

\subsubsection{First ontological collapse: the Newtonian Lagrangian}

The standard Newtonian Lagrangian is obtained by the \textbf{first ontological violation}: the two distinct points \(A\) and \(B\) are forced to coincide, \(r_A = r_B = r\).  This collapses the two‑point structure
into a single‑point function.  Formally, we set \(\kappa_A^{2} =
\kappa^{2}(r)\) and \(\beta_A = 0\), while keeping \(\beta_B = \beta\).
Equation \eqref{eq:two_point_energy} then becomes
\[
\Delta E_{A \to B}
   = \tfrac12\bigl(\kappa^{2}(r) - \kappa^{2}(r)\bigr)
   + \tfrac12\beta^{2}
   = \tfrac12\beta^{2} ,
\]
which no longer contains any potential information.  To recover a potential, one must \emph{separately} introduce the concept of a potential energy function \(V(r)\).  The relational origin of \(V(r)\) is lost; instead it is postulated as \(V(r) = -GMm/r\).  Adding this ad‑hoc potential to the kinetic term yields the familiar Lagrangian
\[
L \;=\; \tfrac12 m v^{2} \;+\; \frac{GMm}{r} ,
\]
which is exactly the expression obtained by taking the clean relational Lagrangian \eqref{eq:L_rel} and arbitrarily setting the constant surface term to \(GMm/r\).  Thus the Newtonian Lagrangian is the degenerate single‑point limit of the two‑point relational law.

and \(\beta\) gives
\[
H \;=\; \tfrac12 m v^{2} \;-\; \frac{GMm}{r} ,
\]
which is the Newtonian Hamiltonian.  The sign change reflects the switch from a surface‑referenced budget (positive potential term) to an infinity‑referenced budget (negative potential term).  Both conventions are contained in the single two‑point law; which one appears depends on which state is taken as the reference.

\subsection{Challenges and Current Limitations}
\hypertarget{sec:challenges}{}\label{sec:challenges}


Gravitational waves and the full radiative two-body problem have not yet been formulated within the relational framework. The current construction addresses only conservative, bound orbits. Extending it to dissipative, radiative dynamics requires a relational description of propagating degrees of freedom on the carriers and remains an open technical task.

A complete, self-consistent multi-body architecture that extends beyond the collinear L1 case has likewise not been constructed. The nested-contour approach provides a clear conceptual route, but its explicit algebraic realisation for systems containing three or more gravitationally bound bodies (including mean-motion resonances and long-term stability) requires further development. The author states openly that progress on both fronts has so far been limited by present mathematical facility rather than by any intrinsic prohibition within the relational ontology. Collaboration with researchers experienced in radiative dynamics and in the algebraic treatment of multi-body closures is therefore actively invited.
