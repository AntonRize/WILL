\begin{abstract}
This supplement to \href{https://willrg.com/documents/WILL_RG_I.pdf}{first part} of the \href{https://willrg.com/documents/WILL_RG_I.pdf#trilogy}{WILL trilogy} presents \textbf{Relational Orbital Mechanics (R.O.M.)} -- a closed algebraic system that classifies allowed bound states of \href{https://willrg.com/documents/WILL_RG_I.pdf#sec:geometric_composition}{relational configuration} without invoking a \href{https://willrg.com/documents/WILL_RG_I.pdf#sec:epicycle}{background spacetime}, a metric tensor, or a mass and gravitational constant.

From the \href{https://willrg.com/documents/WILL_RG_I.pdf#sec:kappabeta}{dimensionless projections \(\beta\) (kinematic) and \(\kappa\) (potential)} on the \href{https://willrg.com/documents/WILL_RG_I.pdf#sec:carriers}{relational carriers \(S^1\) and \(S^2\)}, and from the \href{https://willrg.com/documents/WILL_RG_I.pdf#sec:closure}{closure theorem \(\kappa^2 = 2\beta^2\) } (forced by degree-of-freedom indifference),  all major observed orbital phenomena are derived algebraically as indefinable consequence of Relational Geometry:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Kepler's Third Law, the vis-viva relation, conservation of angular momentum, and the derivation of eccentricity emerge as strict Pythagorean invariants.
    \item Orbital precession is recovered as the accumulation of the relational phase divergence \(\tau_Y^2\), normalized by the orbital shape factor \(1-e^2\), yielding the exact GR value for Mercury without differential equations.
    \item The Schwarzschild radius \(R_s\) and all orbital scales are expressed directly in terms of spectroscopic and chronometric observables (\(z_{\odot}\), \(\theta_{\odot}\), \(T\), \(e\)), bypassing mass \(M\) and Newton's constant \(G\).
    \item Gravitational deflection of massive particles and light follows from the \href{https://willrg.com/documents/WILL_RG_I.pdf#sec:energy-symmetry}{energy symmetry law} with no weak-field expansion.
\end{itemize}


R.O.M. demonstrates that the operational content of gravitational dynamics is fully captured by the algebra of relational projections. The construct serves as a rigorous, empirical realization of the foundational methodological principles established in \href{https://willrg.com/documents/WILL_RG_I.pdf}{WILL Part I Relational Geometry}. As an Open Research all data and calculation tools are publicly available at \href{https://willrg.com/}{willrg.com} and \href{https://github.com/AntonRize/WILL}{https://github.com/AntonRize/WILL}.

\section{Mercury's Precession: GR 1PN as First-Order Approximation of RG}
\hypertarget{sec:mercury}{}\label{sec:mercury}

\subsection{Statement of the Verification Problem}

The standard first-order post-Newtonian expression for the perihelion advance of a bound two-body orbit is
\begin{equation}
\Delta\varphi_{\mathrm{GR}} \;=\; \frac{3\pi\,R_{s}}{a\,(1-e^{2})} ,
\label{eq:dphi-GR}
\end{equation}
with $a$ the semi-major axis, $e$ the eccentricity, and $R_{s} = 2GM/c^{2}$ the Schwarzschild scale of the central body.

WILL Relational Geometry (WILL~RG) expresses the secular angular shift per closed orbit as the strict geometric accumulation of the system's phase divergence from the rest origin:
\begin{equation}
\Delta\varphi_{\mathrm{RG}} \;=\; \frac{2\pi\,\tau_{Y}^{2}}{1-e^{2}} , \qquad \tau_{Y}^{2} \;\equiv\; 1 - \tau^{2} \;=\; 1 - (1-\beta^{2})(1-\kappa^{2}) \;=\; \beta^{2} + \kappa^{2} - \beta^{2}\kappa^{2} ,
\label{eq:dphi-RG}
\end{equation}
evaluated at the orbital reference scale (semi-major axis $a$), where the closure condition $\beta^{2}(a) = \kappa^{2}(a)/2$ and the field relation $\kappa^{2}(a) = R_{s}/a$ hold.

The question addressed in this section is whether \eqref{eq:dphi-GR} is the leading-order content of \eqref{eq:dphi-RG} in the small parameter $R_{s}/a$. The structure of \eqref{eq:dphi-RG} suggests an even stronger statement than in the time-dilation case (\href{https://willrg.com/documents/WILL_RG_I.pdf#sec:earth-gps}{sec:earth-gps}): the GR formula should be recoverable by removing a single explicit term --- the cross-coupling $\beta^{2}\kappa^{2}$ --- from $\tau_{Y}^{2}$, with the residual being exactly $-2\pi\,\beta^{2}\kappa^{2}/(1-e^{2})$.

\subsection{Methodology}

The verification is split into two independent parts, both executed in a single public notebook (Section~\ref{sec:repro-precession}).

\paragraph{Part I --- symbolic identity.} The exact WILL~RG precession is expressed in closed form by substituting the \href{https://willrg.com/documents/WILL_RG_I.pdf#sec:closure}{closure condition $\beta^{2} = \kappa^{2}/2$ }and $\kappa^{2} = R_{s}/a$ into \eqref{eq:dphi-RG}. The result is simplified algebraically and subtracted from \eqref{eq:dphi-GR}. The algebraic identity between the residual and the analytically predicted second-order term $-2\pi\,\beta^{2}\kappa^{2}/(1-e^{2})\big|_{a}$ is verified using \texttt{sympy.simplify}. As an independent cross-check, \eqref{eq:dphi-RG} is Taylor-expanded in a bookkeeping parameter $\varepsilon$ that scales $R_{s}$, and the first-order coefficient is compared with \eqref{eq:dphi-GR}.

\paragraph{Part II --- numerical comparison.} Both \eqref{eq:dphi-GR} and \eqref{eq:dphi-RG} are evaluated with 40-digit precision arithmetic (\texttt{mpmath}, \texttt{mp.dps = 40}) using Mercury's J2000 orbital elements: $a = 5.7909050 \times 10^{10}\,\mathrm{m}$, $e = 0.20563593$, orbital period $T = 87.9691\,\mathrm{d}$, $GM_{\odot} = 1.32712440018 \times 10^{20}\,\mathrm{m^{3}\,s^{-2}}$, $c = 299{,}792{,}458\,\mathrm{m\,s^{-1}}$, giving $R_{s}^{\odot} = 2953.25\,\mathrm{m}$. Per-orbit values are converted to arcseconds per Julian century using $36{,}525\,\mathrm{d}/T$ orbits per century. The numerical discrepancy $\Delta\varphi_{\mathrm{RG}} - \Delta\varphi_{\mathrm{GR}}$ is compared with the analytically predicted second-order correction obtained in Part~I.

\subsection{Results}

\paragraph{Symbolic.} Substituting the closure and field relations into \eqref{eq:dphi-RG} yields the closed-form WILL~RG precession
\begin{equation}

The Taylor expansion of \eqref{eq:dphi-RG} in $R_{s}/a$ reproduces \eqref{eq:dphi-GR} at first order with zero residual.
