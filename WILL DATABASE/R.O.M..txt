\begin{abstract}
This supplement to the \href{https://willrg.com/documents/WILL_RG_I.pdf}{first part} of the \href{https://willrg.com/documents/WILL_RG_I.pdf#trilogy}{WILL trilogy} presents \textbf{Relational Orbital Mechanics (R.O.M.)} -- a closed algebraic system that classifies allowed bound states of \href{https://willrg.com/documents/WILL_RG_I.pdf#sec:geometric_composition}{relational configuration} without invoking a \href{https://willrg.com/documents/WILL_RG_I.pdf#sec:epicycle}{background spacetime}, a metric tensor, or a mass and gravitational constant.

From the \href{https://willrg.com/documents/WILL_RG_I.pdf#sec:kappabeta}{dimensionless projections \(\beta\) (kinematic) and \(\kappa\) (potential)} on the \href{https://willrg.com/documents/WILL_RG_I.pdf#sec:carriers}{relational carriers \(S^1\) and \(S^2\)}, and from the \href{https://willrg.com/documents/WILL_RG_I.pdf#sec:closure}{closure theorem \(\kappa^2 = 2\beta^2\) } (forced by degree-of-freedom indifference),  all major observed orbital phenomena are derived algebraically as indefinable consequence of Relational Geometry:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Kepler's Third Law, the vis-viva relation, conservation of angular momentum, and the derivation of eccentricity emerge as strict Pythagorean invariants.
    \item Orbital precession is recovered as the accumulation of the relational phase divergence \(\tau_Y^2\), normalized by the orbital shape factor \(1-e^2\), yielding the exact GR value for Mercury without differential equations.
    \item The Schwarzschild radius \(R_s\) and all orbital scales are expressed directly in terms of spectroscopic and chronometric observables (\(z_{\odot}\), \(\theta_{\odot}\), \(T\), \(e\)), bypassing mass \(M\) and Newton's constant \(G\).
    \item Gravitational deflection of massive particles and light follows from the \href{https://willrg.com/documents/WILL_RG_I.pdf#sec:energy-symmetry}{energy symmetry law} with no weak-field expansion.
\end{itemize}

The results are tested against the full 24-year astrometric and radial-velocity dataset of the S2 star around Sgr~A*. Using the same parameters as a standard General Relativity 1PN model, the R.O.M. parametrization -- which couples the pericentre advance to the true anomaly rather than to coordinate time -- achieves a \(\Delta\chi^2 \approx -74\) improvement. All orbital scales are extracted from the dimensionless observables alone.

R.O.M. demonstrates that the operational content of gravitational dynamics is fully captured by the algebra of relational projections. The construct serves as a rigorous, empirical realization of the foundational methodological principles established in \href{https://willrg.com/documents/WILL_RG_I.pdf}{WILL Part I Relational Geometry}. As an Open Research all data and calculation tools are publicly available at \href{https://willrg.com/}{willrg.com} and \href{https://github.com/AntonRize/WILL}{https://github.com/AntonRize/WILL}.
\end{abstract}

\kappa^2 + \kappa_X^2 = 1 \quad \Longrightarrow \quad \kappa^2 = 1 - \kappa_X^2.

\end{proof}

evaluated at the orbital reference scale (semi-major axis $a$), where the closure condition $\beta^{2}(a) = \kappa^{2}(a)/2$ and the field relation $\kappa^{2}(a) = R_{s}/a$ hold.
